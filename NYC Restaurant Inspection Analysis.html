NYC Restaurant Inspection Analysis

This analysis examines food safety and regulatory compliance across NYC restaurants based on 933 inspection records from a cleaned dataset. The analysis includes borough-level performance metrics, cuisine-type violation patterns, and temporal trends in inspection scores.

Violation Patterns by Cuisine Type

The analysis identified the top 10 cuisine types with the highest violation counts. Based on the visualization data, American cuisine leads with 147 violations, followed by Chinese (95), Bakery Products/Desserts (67), and Latin American (51). These categories represent the most frequently cited violation types across NYC restaurant inspections.

03

Data Quality and Methodology

The dataset was cleaned from an initial 1,000 inspection records to 933 records by removing entries with missing scores and invalid/placeholder inspection dates (such as 1900-01-01). This 93% data quality retention rate ensures analysis is based on valid inspection results.

Analysis includes:

  • Borough-level aggregation: Average inspection scores grouped by geographic location
  • Cuisine categorization: Violation frequencies by food type served
  • Temporal classification: Year extraction from inspection dates to identify trends
  • Violation flagging: Critical violation identification based on violation description content

The derived dataset contains 29 columns including location identifiers, violation details, inspection metrics, and geographic coordinates enabling multi-dimensional analysis of food safety compliance patterns across NYC.
